about me...

Hi! I’m Layne (she/her). I’m a Bay-Area based, half-brit, neurodiverse, groovy, feeler, thinker, creator, breaker, fixer and healer. I am determined to foster a more safe, kind and lovely society to exist in. I build websites, and I do a lot more than that.

My work ethic recognizes collaboration as an act of abundance, so I can’t wait to hear your vision. I believe the answer to most of life’s questions lives somewhere between radical self-love, authentic identity-informed connection with eachother, a funky beat, and a human-centered digital platform. What does all of that mean? Work with me to find out!


What work do I do?

I believe technology can be a tool to heal, inform, and support community care and environmental health when the right people are making decisions for the greater good.

To me, that could look like me improving an ethically/sustainably sourced home-store website or it could look like me building an app that connects a local community to their activist organizers.


How did I get here?

I worked as a professional communications consultant in the public sector for four years, and found myself drained by the industry-inflicted barriers from helping people. I was ultimately unable to live in integrity with my values. Through that, the world of virtual communication seduced me.

Something you likely don’t know is that us ADHDers are professional problem-solvers (or in the words of Salif Mahamane, we are “potential hunting and gathering badasses”). And…well, I saw a problem and had a solution. I was (and remain to be) heavily concerned about who is leading the exponentially impactful powerhouse that is technology and how it’s being weaponized in society. I see that it is a mechanism for socializing disassociation/dehumanization, classism/income inequality, globalism/anti-localism and detachment from and ultimate destruction of the natural world. As I noted earlier, I also see that tech can be a tool to heal, inform, and support community care & environmental health when the right people are making decisions for the greater good, rather than those centering individualism, power and profit. The power technology has and the power it will have continues to be a threat and an opportunity for all of us.

So I, obviously, had to do what needed doing to get my voice in the room. I knew it was my job to get in there and empower the right people via the virtual world. I attended a 6-month intensive full-stack web development bootcamp and immediately began freelance web development at a cross-section with my strategic communications skills.
